## Scope

The simulations are deterministic synthetic normal-form illustrations. They are not fits to biological, cognitive, artificial, or social systems.

## Key claims

1. Attention is capacity-limited distinction admission into an update channel.
2. Value is causal boundary-gradient relevance under finite capacity.
3. Goals are stabilized value rankings coupled to policies across update windows.
4. Meaning is actionable compressed distinction preserved by a task-sufficient semantic quotient.
5. Strong FDS agency requires updates or actions that causally affect future boundary loss.
6. Self-verifying agency requires internal or coupled verification.
7. Misalignment is divergence between host and delegate action effects on boundary loss.
8. Culture and institutions are shared externalized distinction infrastructures with verification costs.
